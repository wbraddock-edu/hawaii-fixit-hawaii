Hawaii Home Maintenance Checklist for Every Season

Fix-It Hawaii Team · June 2026

Hawaii's tropical climate creates unique home maintenance rhythms unlike anywhere on the mainland. While there aren't dramatic seasonal temperature shifts, Kauai's wet and dry seasons, hurricane season (June through November), and the island's varied microclimates all demand a proactive approach to home maintenance. Here's a comprehensive checklist organized by task frequency.

Monthly Tasks

Test all smoke and carbon monoxide detectors. Check under sinks for any signs of moisture or drip stains. Clear gutters and downspouts — Kauai's rainfall (particularly on the North Shore) can overwhelm clogged drainage quickly. Check your HVAC filters and replace if needed — Hawaii's humidity means filters load up faster than on the mainland. Look for signs of pest activity, particularly termites, which are endemic to Hawaii.

Quarterly Tasks (Every 3 Months)

Inspect your roof for missing shingles, lifted flashing, or debris buildup. Check all exterior caulking around windows, doors, and penetrations — UV exposure and temperature cycling degrade caulk faster in Hawaii. Test your GFCIs (ground fault circuit interrupter outlets) in bathrooms, kitchens, and outdoor areas. Service your water heater — flush the tank if it's tank-style to remove mineral buildup from Hawaii's hard water. Inspect your lanai or deck for soft boards, wobbly railings, or fastener corrosion.

Biannual Tasks (Twice Per Year)

Have your HVAC system professionally serviced. Pressure wash exterior surfaces including walkways, driveway, and siding to remove mold and mildew growth. Check foundation perimeter for water pooling or erosion. Inspect all door and window weatherstripping — replace anything that's cracked or compressed. Service your irrigation system and check for clogged heads or broken lines.

Annual Tasks

Professional pest inspection — especially for termites, which cause an estimated $100 million in Hawaii property damage annually. Full roof inspection by a qualified professional. Re-caulk any exterior joints that show deterioration. Service your garage door opener and springs. Have your electrical panel checked if your home is more than 20 years old.

Hurricane Season Preparation (June–November)

Before hurricane season, trim any dead branches from trees near your home, secure or store outdoor furniture, check your emergency supply kit, and review your homeowner's insurance coverage. For detailed hurricane preparation guidance, see our full hurricane prep guide.
