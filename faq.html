Frequently Asked Questions

Everything Kauai homeowners commonly ask before hiring Fix-It Hawaii.

We serve all of Kauai — from Kapaa and Lihue to Princeville, Hanalei, Poipu, Koloa, and Waimea. For remote locations like the North Shore, there may be a small travel supplement for jobs under $300.

Yes. Fix-It Hawaii is fully licensed as a Specialty Contractor in Hawaii and carries general liability insurance on every job. We can provide a Certificate of Insurance upon request. We never cut corners on licensing — your protection matters.

Use our online booking form or call us directly at (808) 555-0120. For straightforward repairs, we can often provide a phone estimate. For larger jobs, we'll schedule a free on-site assessment. Written estimates are provided before any work begins.

Most small repairs can be scheduled within 3–5 business days. Larger jobs requiring planning and materials may take 1–2 weeks to schedule. Emergency repairs (active leaks, safety issues) receive priority scheduling — call us directly.

We can do it either way. Many homeowners prefer us to supply materials (we buy at trade pricing and pass savings along). Others prefer to supply materials themselves for specific aesthetic choices. Discuss this when requesting your estimate.

We accept cash, check, and major credit cards. For jobs over $1,000, we typically structure payments as 30% at start and 70% upon completion. We never require full payment upfront.

Absolutely — in fact, multi-repair visits are more economical for you. We charge by the job, not the hour, so combining a drywall repair, a leaky faucet, and a ceiling fan installation into one visit saves you both money and scheduling coordination.

When permits are required by Hawaii code, we pull them. Minor repairs, painting, and like-for-like replacements typically don't require permits. We'll always advise you on permitting requirements upfront — we never suggest skipping required permits to save time or money.
